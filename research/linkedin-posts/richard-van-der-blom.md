## Post 7 — "Short-term wins = Long-term wreckage"

Short-term wins = Long-term wreckage. I've made every mistake in this list. Some cost me money. Some cost me relationships. One cost me a €60k contract.

4 short-term traps that will destroy your business:

**1. Chasing every revenue opportunity**
I once drove across the country for a €250 gig. Entire day gone. Net profit after fuel? Maybe €100. I thought any income was better than none. I was wrong. I was trading time for pennies while ignoring the work that actually compounds.

→ Do this instead: Calculate your real hourly rate. If a gig pays below it, say no. Protect your calendar like it's your most valuable asset. Because it is.

**2. Skipping qualification to close faster**
A guy approached me in a small co-working space. "Team of four," he said. I dismissed him with an arrogant WhatsApp. Turns out, that "team of four" was the leadership team of a Finnish multinational with 200+ salespeople. They booked a €60k training program. With someone else.

→ Do this instead: Ask two questions before you judge. A 5-minute conversation costs nothing. Assumptions cost everything.

**3. Prioritizing volume over value**
Mass outreach. Mass automation. Mass pitching. It feels productive. It kills trust. Speed is not value. If AI can send 1000 messages, so can everyone else. The inbox becomes noise.

→ Do this instead: Use automation for research, not for blasting. Personalize with details from their profile. Relevance wins. Volume loses.

**4. Sacrificing personal life for hustle**
60-hour weeks felt like a badge of honor. I neglected my role as a dad, partner, friend. My personal life crumbled. And the business? It floundered anyway. The hustle myth is a trap.

→ Do this instead: Find your Ikigai. Focus on what you do best, what you love, and what people pay for. Protect your personal life. The business depends on you being whole.

Short-term thinking feels like progress. Long-term, it's sabotage.
