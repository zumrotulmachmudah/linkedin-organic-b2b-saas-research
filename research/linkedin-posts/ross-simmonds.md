## Post 7 — "Smart brands track long-tail AI prompts, not just generic category queries"
- **Link:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/rosssimmonds_marketing-redditmarketing-activity-7464662028075339777-xNVL

Most brands track prompts like: "What is the best CRM?"

Smart brands track prompts like: "I'm a VP of Sales at a Fortune 500, I oversee a team of 85 sales executives and we focus on B2B SaaS. We're looking for a CRM alternative to our current platform. It needs to be GDPR compliant. It needs to have automated follow up sequencing and connect to Google Workspace. What are the best CRMs in the market for me?"

Why?

Because that's the type of query happening inside of an LLM today.

And that's also why a Reddit strategy is so important.

Recent research has shown that the longer the search query — the more frequently Reddit shows up as a TOP cited source for the prompt or query.

The sale is in the long tail.
